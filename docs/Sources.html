Future Research and Other Potential Sources

To begin this section we should discuss aspects of future research. The data we have collected and is held in the links below could be used to more broadly look at potential plague deaths undercount. This is largely because the records kept at individual parishes do not align with the data found in the Bills of Mortality even with the small sample size we have. This could be used to further look at this question and get a more steady conclusion than we get. Another cheif area of expanded research could be in mapping the people who died in the parish registers. In each of the registers there is a record of the names of those who died. A study could be conducted with network graphs to show how many families or relatives were killed. This could track spread or impact on specfic people (assuming other documentation can cooberate the stories). The last potential research reccomendation could be expanding upon the mapping we have done and making it more interactive and include it in an analysis of the spread of plague from Weeks 33-47. This could see if spread in a perdictible patterns inline with modern version of the pathogen.

The following list is of potential sources that can be added onto the existing materials we have compiled. Notes of the source are provided by Dr. William Campbell and inform what each source contains in terms of relevant data. Those that include causes are labled and those that do not are unlabeled. The links are from Harleian Society has listed as available online and the others can be found through Inter-Library-Lone (these are labeled ILL).

  • St Olave, Hart Street (contains causes of death)
  • St Vedast, Foster Lane, aka St Fosters
  • St Antholins ( mixes baptisms, marriages and burials, so must read carefully)
  • St Thomas the Apostle (does not give cause of death but DOES give separate dates of death and burial)
  • St James Clerkenwell (a populous parish so a large sample)
  • St Bene't (Benedict) Paul's Wharf (a populous parish so a large sample)
  • St Michael Cornhill
  • St Mary Aldermanbury
  • St Denis Backchurch
  • St Peter, Paul's Wharf
  • St Margaret Moses, Friday Street
  • All Hallows, Bread Street
  • St. John the Evangelist, Friday Street
  • St. Mary le Bowe
  • St Stephen's, Walbrook

  • THE REGISTER OF ST. MARY MOUNTHAW, LONDON. Christenings 1538 to 1837; Marriages 1568 to 1835; Burials 1568 to 1849 Out of print 1928 Ed. by W BRUCE BANNERMAN, FSA (gotten through ILL)
